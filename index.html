All these are brought about through a network of separate legal entities which includes the Shanthimalai Trust, the Shanthimalai Research and Development Trust, as well as other projects. They were originally developed by SMT, later handed over and now independent, e.g. the Shanthimalai Handicraft Society or the Girivalam project. However, we see ourselves as One Family. Since its inception SMT has helped on a large scale to effect social change comprising around 40 villages.


Despite rapid economic development and growth in other parts of the country, this rural area still suffers severe poverty. Drought. Monsoon floods. Death or illness in the family. Tsunami desaster. Job loss. Forces like these can devastate lives that are already difficult.


India experiences longer periods of drought, and more frequently, specifically in the Tiruvannamalai district. And more than anything else, the livelihood of villagers depends on water: farming, construction works, construction jobs almost come down to total zero. Prices for water and food are rocketing. Poor families suffer the most as inflation eats up there anyhow meagre belongings. To recover from a long period of drought, it takes sometimes years for a family, if at all possible.
